The Approach

A structured path
to real improvement

01

Build a solid repertoire

Start with one reliable system as White and one solid response to each of 1.e4 and 1.d4. Consistency matters more than variety at 1500. Every opening tab shows exactly what to play and why — not just the moves, but the plans behind them.

02

Understand structures, not lines

The middlegame section teaches the three structural concepts that drive most positions: outposts, pawn breaks, and the IQP. Once you recognise these in your own games, the right plan becomes visible without calculation.

03

Solve tactical patterns daily

The puzzle bank rotates through 15 positions at three difficulty levels. Click "New Puzzles" to get a fresh set. At 1500, tactical awareness is the single biggest source of rating points — a missed fork costs more than all the opening theory in the world.

04

Convert winning endgames

The three essential endgames — king and pawn opposition, Lucena position, Philidor defence — cover the situations you'll encounter in almost every game that reaches an endgame. Master them and stop throwing away won games.
